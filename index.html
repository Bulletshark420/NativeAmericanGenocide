The Native American Genocide

In the year 1607 England's Jamestown colonists land in present day Virginia, with the goal to settle in the already populated costal side.

Beginning in 1830s the U.S. had undertaken the policy of removing the Native Americans from the east side of the Mississippi river.

Forced Marches and Interments

The Native Americans were forced to march for thousands of miles. Many Native American tribes suffered heavy casualties, The Cherokees had suffered 50 percent fatalities during the “Trail of Tears”

the Choctaws, Chickasaws, Seminoles and Creeks, Had also suffered between 25% to 35% casualties

Discovery of gold at Sutter's mill

In California on January 24th,1848, James W. Marshall found gold at Sutters mill, in the foothills of the Sierra Nevada.

This discovery caused the outbreak of fever that dragged settlers from all corners of the world to California.

Impact of the gold rush on Native Americans.

The massive arrival of settlers caused a violent clash with the inhabitants of the area.

This was the beginning to what was improperly called the "Indian Wars" which lased well into the 20th century.

Indian Wars

The Indian Wars were a series of conflicts between American Indians and European settlers that took place from the colonial period to the late 19th century.

Colonial Period Indian Wars

In 1622 the Powhatan Indians attacked and killed colonists in eastern Virginia, This is known as the Jamestown Massacre.

In 1636 The Pequot war began which was a Conflict between Pequot Indians and English settlers of the Massachusetts Bay Colony and Connecticut.

In 1640 until 1701 the Beaver wars were going on, it was Fierce fighting between the French and their Indian allies and the Iroquois Confederacy over territory, and fur trade dominance around the Great Lakes.

In 1675-1676 there was the King Philip war, the Wampanoag Chief Metacom led attacks on colonies and militia strongholds throughout Massachusetts and Rhode Island.

In 1702-1713 was Queen Anne's War, Conflict between French and English colonists and their respective Indian allies on several fronts.

In 1711-1715 was the Tuscarora War, Tuscarora Indians burned North Carolina settlements and randomly killed colonists over treaty disputes.

In 1715 the Yamasee War happened, Yamasee Indians formed a confederacy and attacked South Carolina settlers over the loss of their hunting grounds and high debts.

Nineteenth-Century Wars

In 1816-1858 there was the Seminole Wars, which was Conflict between the United States and the Seminole tribe in Florida.

In 1864 was the Sand Creek Massacre, Colorado Territory militia attacked and killed peaceful Cheyenne and Arapaho Indians.

In 1876 the Battle of the Little Bighorn Conflict between the U.S. Army and Northern Plains tribes led by Sitting Bull and Crazy Horse.

in 1890 U.S. soldiers killed more than 150 Sioux Indians, mostly women and children This was called the Wounded Knee.

The impact of the Indian wars Led to the loss of land, culture, and lives for American Indians, It also Helped to shape the United States as a nation.

Trail Of Tears

The Trail Of Tears was a forced relocation of Native Americans from southeastern United States to Indian Territory (present-day Oklahoma) in the 1830s.

Over 125,000 Native Americans affected, resulting in the death and suffering of thousands.

This was also known as the "Indian Removal"

Why did the trail of tears begin

The Native Americans lived on valuable land that the settlers wanted for cotton farming

The settlers used violent means to take land from Native Americans

State governments had also passed laws limiting Native American sovereignty and rights

On Nov. 29, 1864, more than 150 Cheyenne and Arapaho, including many women and children, were brutally killed by more than 700 soldiers.
